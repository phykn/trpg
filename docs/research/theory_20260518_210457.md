# Cycle 5: Relation as a Primitive

## Claim

`GroundedAffordanceState` can be reduced to six primitives:

- Actor
- Locus
- Resource
- Commitment
- PendingProcess
- Binding

Definitions:

- `Actor`: entity that can act or be targeted.
- `Locus`: place/topology determining presence and movement.
- `Resource`: possessable, usable, equippable, consumable capability or stat-like quantity.
- `Commitment`: quest, goal, or obligation that changes valid actions or success conditions.
- `PendingProcess`: unresolved confirmation, roll, combat, or process that controls the next step.
- `Binding`: authoritative reference from language/context to concrete ids/actions.

## Evidence

- `server/src/game/domain/graph/models.py:6-22` defines many node types, including character, item, location, quest, skill, effect, status, faction, action, and knowledge.
- `server/src/game/domain/graph/models.py:24-49` defines relation types including `located_at`, `carries`, `equips`, `connects_to`, `relation`, `uses_effect`, `uses_action`, and `has_knowledge`.
- `server/src/game/domain/graph/validation.py:6-31` constrains edge endpoints by node types.
- `server/src/game/runtime/action/dispatch.py:53-90` dispatches noncombat actions through planned `GraphChange`s and progress updates.
- `server/src/game/runtime/action/dispatch.py:132-171` maps move and quest accept/abandon to graph changes plus progress changes.
- `server/src/game/engines/graph/quest.py:79-127` validates quest state transitions.
- `server/src/game/engines/graph/quest.py:150-180` progresses quests by triggers.

## Objection

The six primitives lose social relation state.

`relation`, `member_of_faction`, and `faction_relation` do not fit cleanly into Actor, Resource, Commitment, PendingProcess, or Binding. A relation is not merely an actor property and not merely a resource. It is a transition condition between actors or factions.

Additional evidence:

- `server/src/game/domain/graph/models.py:39` defines `relation` as an edge type; `server/src/game/domain/graph/models.py:43-44` define faction membership and faction relation edge types.
- `server/src/game/domain/graph/validation.py:21` validates `relation` as character-to-character.
- `server/src/game/runtime/flow/roll.py:631-644` reads relation affinity and changes the effective roll DC.
- `server/src/game/runtime/flow/roll.py:647-681` applies roll relation effects by creating or updating a `relation` edge.
- `server/tests/game/seed/test_seed_validation.py:239-259` rejects unknown faction references and faction relation targets.

## Revision

Add `Relation` as a primitive.

Revised formula:

```text
GroundedAffordanceState =
Actor + Locus + Resource + Relation + Commitment + PendingProcess + Binding
```

`Relation` means social or rule-bearing state between actors or factions. It changes action availability, roll difficulty, NPC response, or success conditions.

## Why It Is Stronger

The revision keeps the compression but restores a missing decision boundary.

It prevents `Resource` from becoming an overbroad bucket for items, stats, skills, status, effects, and social affinity. It also explains why relation edges and faction relations are validated as first-class graph facts.
