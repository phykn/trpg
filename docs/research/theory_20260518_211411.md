# Cycle 8: Continuity Is Not Only Presentation

## Claim

`ContinuityContext` has reduced to bounded presentation memory:

- recent dialogue
- related memory
- important history
- visible narration/log tail

All authoritative truth, grounding, and process control belongs in `GroundedAffordanceState`.

## Evidence

- `docs/research/THEORY.md` includes `GroundedAffordanceState` primitives for Actor, Locus, Possession, Capability, Meter, Attribute, EffectState, Relation, Knowledge, Commitment, PendingProcess, and Binding.
- `server/src/game/runtime/narration/result.py:123-140` persists `turn_log` and `recent_dialogue` from narration result, not through `GraphChange`.
- `server/src/game/runtime/narration/memory_context.py:12-23` selects important history by importance and limit.
- `server/src/game/runtime/narration/memory_context.py:34-43` limits classify recent dialogue.
- `server/src/game/runtime/narration/memory_context.py:46-56` limits narrate recent dialogue.
- `server/src/game/runtime/narration/memory_context.py:88-114` selects related memory by related ids and importance with a limit.

## Objection

The claim is too strong.

`ContinuityContext` is bounded, but not merely presentational. Some bounded memory is non-authoritative for state transitions, yet authoritative enough to constrain future narration and dialogue.

Additional evidence:

- `server/src/game/runtime/narration/result.py:149-158` stores `turn_summary` as `TurnLogEntry.summary`.
- `server/src/game/runtime/narration/result.py:130-140` appends history and dialogue entries outside graph changes.
- `server/src/game/runtime/narration/memory_context.py:88-114` reuses related memory as a payload.
- `server/src/locale/prompts/graph_narrate/prompt.ko.md:149-153` says NPC answers must stay inside target view, public knowledge, available items, related memory, current place, and current result.
- `server/src/locale/prompts/graph_narrate/prompt.ko.md:253-263` defines turn summary and importance, including clues, relation changes, meaningful responses, quests, danger, promises, location clues, and combat results.
- `server/tests/game/runtime/test_memory_context.py:51-70` verifies related memory prefers relevance before importance.

## Revision

Split authority into transition authority and narration authority.

Revised formula:

```text
MinConditions = GroundedAffordanceState + ContinuityContext

GroundedAffordanceState = transition-authoritative
ContinuityContext = bounded narration-authoritative
```

`ContinuityContext` has two subtypes:

- `PresentationMemory`: repetition, style, visible narration/log tail.
- `NarrativeMemory`: bounded truth-bearing summaries used to preserve dialogue and narration continuity.

## Why It Is Stronger

The revision avoids forcing all truth into graph state.

It explains why `related_memory` and `turn_log` are persisted and reused even though they are bounded and not applied through `GraphChange`.
