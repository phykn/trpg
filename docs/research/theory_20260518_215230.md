# Theory Cycle 18

## Claim

Reason category alone is not enough. Minimum invalidity legibility must expose:

1. operative constraint type;
2. current blocking or allowing condition;
3. one repair path, or confirmation that no repair path is currently available.

It should not expose full rules, hidden facts, exact DCs, secret clocks, or exhaustive alternatives.

## Evidence

- "Too hard" is weak for jumping a chasm while carrying someone. "Blocked by load and distance; dropping weight, finding a bridge, or spending a resource could help" is stronger.
- "Cannot equip item" is weak. "Requires Strength 14" is enough when Strength is visible.
- "Story condition unmet" can be enough when revealing the exact condition would spoil a mystery, but the player still needs a frontier such as investigate, gain access, or accept no current route.
- "The iron gate is locked; you need a key or another way around" gives constraint, condition, and repair without listing every command.

## Objection

The claim is close, but "current blocking or allowing condition" can demand hidden facts and collapse mystery.

Also, "repair path" applies cleanly to blocked transitions but not accepted ones. For accepted transitions, the counterpart is counterfactual invalidity: what kind of thing would have blocked this move.

## Revision

For nontrivial accepted or blocked transitions, minimum invalidity legibility requires:

```text
NonSpoilingContrastiveFrontier
```

Participants must be able to tell:

- what class of constraint governed the transition;
- what visible or safely revealable condition made it allowed or blocked;
- what adjacent change would alter validity, unless no such change is currently available.

For blocked transitions, expose one possible repair or say no repair is currently available.

For accepted transitions, expose the relevant blocker class when acceptance might otherwise look arbitrary.

## Why it is stronger

The revision does not require full rules, hidden facts, or exhaustive alternatives.

It makes validity contestable:

```text
If I changed X, would the answer change?
```

It also protects mystery by allowing hidden fact categories without revealing hidden facts.

## Next question

Stable enough. Future loops should test edge cases such as mystery, social manipulation, fate, and GM fiat, but the core structure is unlikely to change.
