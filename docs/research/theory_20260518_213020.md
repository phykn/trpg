# Theory Cycle 12

## Claim

Computed affordances like `can_attack` and `protected=true` should be a narrower grounding-constraints subcategory inside `TransitionState`, not ordinary world facts and not `ContinuityContext`.

## Evidence

- `server/src/llm/context/classify_view.py` computes `can_attack` under `affordances`.
- `_attackable_ids` excludes targets marked `protected`.
- Visible target payloads include `protected=true` when the graph node has that property.
- `server/src/llm/calls/classify/grounding.py` collects protected and attackable ids from the grounding view and rejects protected attacks.
- `server/src/locale/prompts/classify/prompt.ko.md` tells the classifier not to attack `protected=true` targets.
- Tests assert `can_attack` excludes protected targets and that protected target attacks are refused or rejected.

## Objection

The claim overreaches on `protected=true`.

`can_attack` is a computed grounding constraint. But `protected=true` originates as a graph node property, so it is a `TransitionState` world fact with a grounding consequence.

## Revision

Computed affordances such as `can_attack` are derived grounding constraints.

Facts such as `protected=true` remain `TransitionState` source facts. They become grounding-relevant when projected into `GroundingView`.

They are not `ContinuityContext`.

## Why it is stronger

The revision separates source facts from derived constraints without adding a new minimum condition. It explains why the same fact can be durable world state and also shape the classifier/grounding boundary.

## Next question

Stable enough. Further cycles are likely to rename `identity` and `affordances` rather than change the theory's decisions, predictions, or boundaries.
