# Cycle 6: Resource Is a Family

## Claim

`Resource` should remain a single primitive inside `GroundedAffordanceState`.

It covers inventory objects, equipment, skills/capabilities, HP/MP, stats, status, gold, XP, item effects, and similar quantities because all of them constrain grounded action availability or outcomes.

## Evidence

- `server/src/wire/models.py:63-104` separates HP/MP resource payloads, stats, equipment, inventory, status, and skills in hero front state.
- `server/src/wire/models.py:158-165` represents combat supports as skill-based supports with `mp_cost` and `usable`.
- `server/src/wire/graph/character.py:19-34` extracts stats and status from character properties.
- `server/src/wire/graph/character.py:37-47` extracts known skills.
- `server/src/wire/graph/character.py:50-80` extracts equipment and inventory.
- `server/src/wire/graph/combat.py:70-90` computes available skill supports from known skills and current MP.
- `server/src/game/engines/graph/item_use.py:35-79` requires a carried item, applies effects, and removes a consumable carry edge.
- `server/src/game/engines/graph/item_use.py:186-199` requires carried-by and effect nodes.
- `server/src/game/engines/graph/combat.py:145-160` requires a known skill and supported action.
- `server/src/game/engines/graph/combat.py:186-204` requires a carried/equipped item and supported action.

## Objection

The claim over-compresses.

These values all constrain grounded actions, but they constrain them in different ways:

- inventory/equipment: ownership and placement decide targets and use availability
- skill/capability: learned or granted abilities decide action support
- HP/MP/gold/XP: meters decide cost, survival, growth, and trade
- stats: attributes decide roll thresholds and derived success chances
- status/effect: conditions and effects decide transformations and applicable modifiers

Additional evidence:

- `server/src/game/domain/graph/models.py:6-49` gives items, skills, effects, status, and their edges distinct ontology slots.
- `server/src/game/domain/graph/validation.py:6-31` validates different endpoint types for `carries`, `equips`, `knows_skill`, `uses_effect`, `applies_status`, and `uses_action`.
- `server/src/game/domain/graph/query.py:164-184` queries inventory, equipment, and skills through different edge paths.
- `server/src/game/runtime/flow/roll.py:582-585` reads stats for roll construction.
- `server/src/game/engines/graph/transfer.py:86-93` treats item placement and equipment as transfer constraints.

## Revision

Demote `Resource` from primitive to family.

Revised formula:

```text
GroundedAffordanceState =
Actor + Locus + Possession + Capability + Meter + Attribute + EffectState
+ Relation + Commitment + PendingProcess + Binding
```

Definitions:

- `Possession`: item ownership, equipment, and placement.
- `Capability`: learned, granted, or item-provided action support.
- `Meter`: consumable or accumulative quantities such as HP, MP, gold, and XP.
- `Attribute`: stable values used for checks and derived results, such as stats and level.
- `EffectState`: statuses, effects, and applied modifiers.

`ResourceFamily` can remain a convenience name for these five primitives, but it is not itself a primitive.

## Why It Is Stronger

The revision preserves compression while restoring decision power.

It can now answer which minimum condition is missing: possession, capability, meter, attribute, or effect state. That is more useful than saying only that a resource is missing.
