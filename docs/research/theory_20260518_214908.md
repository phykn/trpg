# Theory Cycle 16

## Claim

The minimum validity evidence is `InvalidityLegibility`.

For any accepted transition, participants must be able to infer the class of constraint that allowed it and the class of condition that would have blocked it.

## Evidence

Trust often survives without full rule explanation when players can recognize the relevant gate:

- fictional position,
- resource,
- risk,
- leverage,
- authority,
- consent,
- genre constraint.

Trust fails when actions are accepted or blocked with no stable visible reason.

## Objection

The claim is too broad and too abstract.

It is too broad because trivial transitions do not need explicit contrast. Walking through an open door or answering a greeting should not require rule bookkeeping.

It is too abstract because almost any transition can receive a post-hoc constraint label. If the system can invent the reason after the fact, the theory becomes unfalsifiable.

Timing also matters. A reason exposed before or during resolution preserves more trust than a reason invented only after objection.

## Revision

Minimum validity evidence is:

```text
ChallengeResponsiveInvalidityLegibility
```

For any nontrivial accepted transition, the system must expose enough evidence that, if challenged, participants can identify:

- the operative constraint type;
- one plausible blocking condition.

This does not require full rule bookkeeping.

Evidence can stay implicit for trivial transitions. It must become explicit when the transition is risky, costly, contested, surprising, irreversible, or challenged.

## Why it is stronger

This is more falsifiable:

```text
What kind of thing made this allowed?
What kind of thing would have stopped it?
```

If participants cannot answer those questions for a nontrivial transition, validity was not exposed enough.

## Next question

Which transitions count as nontrivial enough to require explicit invalidity legibility: only challenged transitions, or all risky, costly, surprising, contested, or irreversible transitions even before anyone objects?
